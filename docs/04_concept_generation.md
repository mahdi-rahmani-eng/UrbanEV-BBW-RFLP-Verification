# 04 — Concept Generation

## Purpose

This document explains how the three low-speed urban EV concepts were generated before concept selection.

The objective is to show that the vehicle concepts are not arbitrary. They represent different design trade-offs between battery size, motor power, vehicle mass, maximum speed, range, braking performance, and implementation feasibility.

---

## Link to Product Development Process

In the concept-development process, product concepts should be generated before they are selected.

In this project, three simplified vehicle concepts are generated to represent different possible solutions for the same mission:

> A low-speed urban EV suitable for campus or short urban mobility, extended with concept-level emergency braking verification.

---

## Design Variables Used for Concept Generation

The concepts are generated by varying the following design variables:

| Design Variable | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Battery capacity | Influences estimated range and vehicle mass. |
| Motor power | Influences acceleration potential, speed capability, and system adequacy. |
| Vehicle mass | Influences range, braking distance, and feasibility. |
| Maximum speed | Defines the low-speed operating envelope. |
| CdA | Simplified aerodynamic drag parameter. |
| Crr | Rolling resistance coefficient. |
| Implementation simplicity | Qualitative feasibility for a university-level concept. |

|---|---|---|

---

## Design Rationale

### Concept C1 — Campus-focused Lightweight EV

C1 is generated as the simplest and lightest concept. It is suitable for low-speed campus operation, where maximum speed and range demand are limited. Its low mass is beneficial for braking performance and implementation simplicity. However, its limited battery and motor power reduce its suitability for broader urban operation.

### Concept C2 — Balanced Urban EV

C2 is generated as the balanced concept. It uses moderate battery capacity, moderate motor power, and moderate mass. It is expected to provide a better compromise between range, braking performance, feasibility, and urban usability.

### Concept C3 — Extended Urban EV

C3 is generated as the most capable concept in terms of battery capacity and motor power. It can potentially provide higher range and speed capability, but this comes with higher mass, greater braking demand, and higher implementation complexity.

---

## Link to Concept Selection

The generated concepts are evaluated in the next step using MATLAB-based range estimation, braking-distance analysis, motor adequacy scoring, mass scoring, and feasibility judgement.

The goal of concept selection is not only to select the strongest concept in one criterion, but to identify the most balanced concept for the defined mission.

---

## Scope Note

The generated concepts are simplified university-level design alternatives. They are not production vehicle platforms and do not claim detailed industrial vehicle sizing.
